# Industry — SAP / S/4HANA Modernization

Reusable market context for any pitch or content aimed at the SAP-modernization buyer. (First
landed via the Nova Intelligence work; generalize as we learn more.)

## The deadline that drives everything
SAP is ending mainstream maintenance for legacy **ECC / Business Suite 7** (the "2027" cutoff,
with extended support to ~2030). Every large SAP shop must move to **S/4HANA**. This is the rare
enterprise-software dynamic: a **board-level, externally-enforced clock.** It pulls buyers to the
table — the lowest-CAC motion in enterprise marketing is catching deadline-driven demand, not
manufacturing it.

## The hard part: custom code
The migration's long pole isn't the platform — it's the **custom ABAP** every enterprise has
piled onto SAP over decades. "Clean core" means stripping/replacing those customizations with
governed extensions. Reading, mapping, and modernizing that custom estate is where budgets and
timelines blow up — and where agentic-Ai modernization tools (e.g., Nova) make their claim.

## Vocabulary (so copy sounds native)
**S/4HANA · ECC · RISE with SAP · ABAP · clean core · CDS views · module interdependency ·
codebase scan · migration map.**

## The buying committee
- **CIO / VP of IT / SAP Program Director** — economic buyer; cares about timeline, cost, board risk.
- **SAP COE lead** — champion; cares about team throughput and the scarcity of ABAP talent.
- **Enterprise / ABAP architect** — evaluator; will only believe a **live demo on their own code**, not a demo dataset.
- **Finance / transformation lead** — influencer; wants measurable ROI.

## Where these buyers gather (event surface, DACH-led)
DSAG (German SAP user group), SAP TechEd, SAP Sapphire, SPS Nuremberg (automation), LogiMAT
(intralogistics), Hannover Messe (cross-vertical industrial). The DACH cluster reaches both the
SAP buying committee and industrial leadership in a tight calendar.

## Messaging that lands
- To the CIO: *"cut the custom-code phase — the part that blows up budgets and dates."*
- To the COE lead: *"triple your team's throughput without hiring scarce ABAP talent."*
- To the architect: *proof over promise — run live on their actual repository.*
- To finance: *hard ROI (e.g., ~75% less manual effort, ~50% cost reduction).*

## Content angle
The deadline is the story. Position around **catching** the board-level pressure every SAP CIO is
already under, and around proof on *their* code — not generic AI claims.
