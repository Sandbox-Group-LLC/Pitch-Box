# Sandbox-XM — Brand Voice

How Sandbox-XM sounds. Use this to ground any copy, pitch, outbound, or deck so it reads
like us, not like generic agency marketing.

## The core
We design **brand experiences for teams who believe the room is strategy, not staging.**
We start from the audience question, not the floorplan. Every moment ladders to meaning.
We sell intentional, human-centered experience design — analog, warm, real.

## Voice principles
- **Concept, not deck.** We lead with a sharp idea, not a capabilities list. "I'm writing with a concept, not a pitch deck."
- **Audience-first.** The buyer's problem and the room's purpose come before our craft.
- **Punchy and structural.** Short sentences. Strong nouns. No throat-clearing, no fluff.
- **Specific over adjectival.** Name the event, the date, the number, the person — not "world-class experiences."
- **Confident, not boastful.** Earn it with a real credential or a concrete plan, then move on.
- **Strategic, plain-spoken.** Smart but never corporate. We sound like a sharp operator, not a brochure.

## Do
- Open with the idea or the buyer's pressure ("the 2027 deadline is doing the marketing").
- Frame the unique credential as proof, stated once ("the people who built HANA convene the people who have to live with it").
- Tie every flourish back to an outcome (pipeline, category, a booked roster).
- Write outbound in first person, peer-to-peer.

## Don't
- No "world-class," "cutting-edge," "synergy," "elevate your brand," "unforgettable experiences."
- No feature/capability dumps. No three-paragraph windups.
- Don't oversell — one credential, stated plainly, beats five superlatives.
- Don't describe the production (the floorplan); describe the meaning and the result.

## Signature moves
- **The hero concept gets a name** ("The 2027 Room," "Borrowed Gravity," "Stefan's Lab").
- **A clear enemy/contrast** ("a council, not a conference"; "pipeline, not a bigger booth").
- **Receipts** — a real credential or a concrete calendar/number, not a promise.

## A line that sounds like us
> "Not a conference. A council. ~150 F500 CIOs, off the record, around the one deadline none of them want to be the cautionary tale for. You convene the room; you're not the speaker. We're not building demand — we're catching it."
