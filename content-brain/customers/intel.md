## Snapshot
- **What:** Intel Corporation — the world's largest semiconductor manufacturer. Designs and manufactures CPUs (Xeon, Core), accelerators (Gaudi), GPUs (Arc), foundry services (Intel Foundry), and a broad portfolio spanning data center, client computing, networking, and automotive.
- **Ticker:** NASDAQ: INTC
- **CEO:** Lip-Bu Tan (turnaround mandate: engineering excellence, customer trust, financial discipline)
- **Category position:** Pivoting from legacy x86 CPU company → the trusted **enterprise AI infrastructure advisor**. Broadest silicon portfolio in the industry (Xeon + Gaudi + Arc + Foundry), competing against NVIDIA's GPU monoculture and AMD's growing data center share.
- **Why they matter to us:** Intel is Sandbox-XM's **anchor client** — the largest, longest-running, and most operationally complex account. The relationship spans owned tentpole events, third-party activations, keynote content production, creative strategy, and partner event design.

## The Relationship — How Sandbox-XM Serves Intel

### Engagement model
Sandbox-XM (operating as Sandbox Group LLC) is Intel's **lead experience agency** for the Events & Experiences (E&E) organization. The primary Intel stakeholder is the E&E team led by Deidre Rippy. Sandbox-XM handles:

- **Experience strategy & creative** — overall event strategy, creative direction, visual identity, attendee journey, environmental design, floor plans, and renders.

- **Third-party event creative** — designing Intel's presence at partner/industry events (CES, MWC, Embedded World, Computex, HPE Discover, etc.) per the Partner Events Playbook.

### Agency partnership — Sandbox-XM + Taylor Inc.
Intel's event delivery operates through a **dual-agency model**:
- **Sandbox-XM** owns strategy, creative, production management, venue/vendor logistics, technology, and content.
- **Taylor Inc.** owns design execution, fabrication, printing, physical build, AV equipment/crew, and keynote/breakout room technical production.
- Taylor is Intel's preferred fabricator and exhibit rental provider with warehoused assets in Las Vegas and Toronto.
- Financially, Intel issues POs to Taylor, who passes through Sandbox-XM scope costs until Sandbox becomes a direct Intel supplier.

## Intel's Strategic Posture (Public)

### The Lip-Bu Tan turnaround
Tan's mandate: engineering excellence, customer trust, financial discipline. Demands a **smaller, sharper, customer-led** event format — the opposite of the legacy tentpole-at-scale playbook.

### AI infrastructure pivot
Intel is repositioning from "the CPU company" to **"the enterprise AI infrastructure advisor."** Key competitive dynamics:
- **vs. NVIDIA:** Contesting the cost-per-token narrative and CUDA lock-in. Intel's Gaudi 3 vs. H100/H200 benchmarks are the battleground. Intel's structural moat: it can host sovereign AI conversations that NVIDIA cannot (China exposure).
- **vs. AMD:** AMD's "only viable alternative" claim is contestable. Intel's portfolio breadth (Xeon + Gaudi + Arc + Foundry) reframes AMD's mono-architecture as a limitation.
- **Off-CUDA migration:** A $2B+ whitespace where Intel is currently invisible. PyTorch → oneAPI migration is the developer wedge.

### Buyer personas Intel is targeting
- **CIO / VP IT** — AI fleet refresh decisions, data center modernization
- **Chief AI Officer** — brand-new C-suite role with no established playbook
- **Government/Defense AI Systems Integrators** — sovereign AI infrastructure
- **Hyperscaler infra leads** — cloud provider silicon decisions
- **AI-native CTOs** — startup/scale-up AI infrastructure
- **Developers** — off-CUDA migration, oneAPI adoption

### Automotive BU
Intel Automotive is a dedicated BU with renewed ambitions post-Mobileye IPO. "Intel is inside 50 million cars on the road today." Strategy: drive the automotive industry's transformation toward connected, software-defined, electrified vehicles. Roadmap: Apollo Lake (current) → Raptor Lake (2024-25) → Grizzly Lake (2027+, first purpose-built automotive product). Differentiator: open architecture, workload consolidation via virtualization, and IFS chiplet advantage.
