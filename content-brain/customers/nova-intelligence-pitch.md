## ★ Headline Pitch A — **The 2027 Room**

**Play:** Signature Activation Pitch

### One-liner
> A closed-door **council** — not a conference — for the F500 CIOs and architects landing their S/4HANA migration before SAP's **2027 ECC deadline**. Two parallel tracks (Marcus / strategic + Stefan / technical), an annual peer-benchmark artifact (**The 2027 Migration Index**), and a credential moment only Nova can stage (HANA co-inventor in conversation with a customer CIO). The category-creation room **AND** the F500 pipeline funnel, fused in one architecture.

### The frame — why this format, why now
Not a conference. Not a user group. **A council** — ~150 invite-only Fortune 500 CIOs, VPs of IT, SAP Program Directors, and Enterprise Architects, gathered twice a year, off the record, around a single shared problem: SAP's **2027 ECC maintenance deadline** is 18 months away, every one of them is mid-migration, and none of them want to be the cautionary tale.

> **Nova convenes the room. Nova is not the speaker. Nova is the operating layer beneath the room.**

The 2027 deadline is the rarest thing in enterprise software: a **board-level, externally-enforced clock.** Every CIO on the invite list is already under pressure to attend a room exactly like this. *We are not building demand — we are catching it.* And the competitive frame is clean: no SI, no consulting firm, and no other AI vendor can convene this room, because none of them have Nova's credential stack. **The 2027 Room is the only room where the people who BUILT HANA convene the people who have to LIVE WITH it.**

### The two-track structure
Two named personas — different humans, different jobs — run parallel for two days. Joint plenary opens day one; joint dinner closes day two.

- **🟦 The Marcus Track** — *SAP Program Directors / VPs of IT.* The strategic frame: budget defense, vendor-selection methodology, board reporting templates, and anonymized peer benchmarks ("here's where the F500 actually is on the migration curve, six months ahead of the public narrative"). **Marcus walks out with a deck he can present to his CEO on Monday.**
- **🟧 The Stefan Track** — *SAP Enterprise Architects.* Architect-grade technical sessions: **live custom-code teardowns on attendees' own ABAP repositories** — Nova's AI runs the POC live, in the room, on real code. Clean Core governance workshops, CDS view migration patterns, module interdependency mapping. **Stefan walks out having seen Nova handle his actual codebase, not a demo dataset.**

> The design point: Marcus shows up for the strategic frame, Stefan shows up for the technical proof, and they go home aligned for the first time — because they were aligned in the same building.

### The artifact — The 2027 Migration Index
The single most defensible piece of IP Nova can own. An annual, anonymized aggregate dataset built from every attendee's **pre-event codebase scan** (Nova runs the scans; attendees opt in to inclusion). It becomes the authoritative answer to the question every SAP-shop CIO is asking and no one has answered: *how far along is the F500, actually?* Published as a paid report; excerpts to **The Wall Street Journal** and **CIO Magazine**; methodology lives at Nova.

> **In year one, the Index is the room. In year two, the room joins because of the Index.**

### The credential moment
Not a product demo. A fireside between Nova's **SAP HANA co-inventor** and a customer CIO mid-migration, on stage, on the record:

> **"What HANA was for, and what migration was supposed to be."**

This is the moment press writes about — clipped and circulated for 12 months after. It makes Nova's credential stack physical, in front of the buyer, with no Salesforce-style spin. *Microsoft, Salesforce, Deloitte, Accenture structurally cannot replicate it — they don't have the human.*

### The pipeline tail
> **Pattern A is the strategy. Pipeline is the receipt.**

Every attendee gets a pre-event codebase scan — that **is** the invite mechanism. Every Marcus walks out with a board-ready migration narrative; every Stefan walks out having seen Nova handle his code. Structured follow-up owned by the Nova GTM team, mapped to deal stages. Two rooms a year, each capable of seeding **12–18 months of named enterprise pipeline.** The 2027 Room is the room, the brand, and the funnel — in one architecture.

### Why it wins for Nova
- **Hard external deadline pulls the buyer to the room** — the lowest-CAC enterprise marketing motion possible.
- **Two-track format serves both buying personas simultaneously** — the deal-shape Nova closes (technical + executive sponsor) is mirrored in the room itself.
- **The Index is a structural moat** — by year two, no competitor can publish a credible alternative.
- **The credential moment is unrepeatable** — Microsoft, Salesforce, Deloitte, Accenture do not have the human.
- **Anti-conference, pro-signal** — the opposite of SAPPHIRE NOW. Exactly the brand Nova should want.
- **Category capture** — *"agentic AI for SAP"* gets named, defined, and authority-tagged in the room before Microsoft or a consulting giant tries to rename it.

## The Negotiation Tail — Four Quick Ideas

Each maps to a specific objection Emma is likely to raise. Kept as history (`status='sketch'`) — *leverage, not B-tier ideas.*

### 1. The 2027 Index — *"the full Room is too expensive for our stage"*
**Artifact-only — no room, no event.** An annual published benchmark on F500 S/4HANA migration progress that becomes the authoritative number in a category no one else owns.

Nova runs anonymized codebase scans for ~75–100 F500 SAP shops over the year, aggregates the data, and publishes *"The 2027 Migration Index: Where the Fortune 500 Actually Is."* Excerpts to WSJ and CIO Magazine. Methodology lives at Nova.
- Dramatically lower cost than the full Room (no venue, travel, or production).
- Still captures the structural moat — by year two, no competitor can publish a credible alternative.
- Still positions Nova as the category-defining authority on *"agentic AI for SAP."*
- Builds the invite list for a future 2027 Room — every participating CIO becomes a warm contact.
- **By year two, Nova owns** the number every analyst, banker, and competitor cites when discussing SAP modernization.

### 2. Stefan's Lab — *"we need wins with architects, not CIOs"*
**A quarterly architect-only salon** — 30 SAP Enterprise Architects in a closed room running Nova on their own ABAP code. Pure technical credibility, zero CIO theater.

Off-the-record, single venue (NYC or Bay Area), single day. The entire format is one thing: Nova's AI running live on attendees' actual ABAP repositories, with the architects who wrote that code in the seats. Clean Core governance workshops, CDS view teardowns, module interdependency mapping. No keynotes, no marketing.
- Stefan-only — drops Marcus, cutting venue cost, run-of-show complexity, and follow-up roughly in half.
- The deepest technical credibility possible; architect-to-architect peer validation is the hardest currency to fake or replicate.
- Quarterly cadence = a year-round drumbeat of in-product POCs — each Lab generates 30 named technical evaluations (the early-stage deal-shape Nova closes on).
- **By year two, Nova owns** a standing community of the ~120 most influential SAP Enterprise Architects in the F500 — all of whom have personally seen Nova run on their own code.

### 3. The Co-Inventor Series — *"we need brand, not events"*
**A long-form documentary content play** — five short films a year with Nova's SAP HANA co-inventor in conversation with the original HANA team and customer CIOs. Brand play, not pipeline play.

Five 15–20 minute films/year, festival-circuit-quality, distributed where the SAP buyer lives (LinkedIn, CIO Magazine's video channel, the SAPInsider community, YouTube). One annual NYC (or Half Moon Bay) screening that doubles as a press moment.
- Pure brand-building. No venue, no two-day production. Sandbox-XM owns the producing relationship end-to-end so Nova's marketing team stays on growth.
- The credential *is* the format — no one else has a SAP HANA co-inventor available for long-form, on-the-record conversation.
- Compounds — year-one films are evergreen and keep educating buyers for years.
- **By year two, Nova owns** the definitive video record of SAP modernization, told from the inside. *"Watch the Co-Inventor series"* becomes the sales-enablement opener for every enterprise deal.

### 4. The Migration Room — *"show me how this drives revenue, not brand"*
**A monetizable single-customer service offering** — Nova goes on-site to a F500 customer mid-migration for a one-day teardown of their actual codebase with 15–20 of their internal stakeholders.

One day, on-site, 15–20 stakeholders in the room (CIO, SAP Program Director, lead Enterprise Architects, the integration partner). Nova runs a live teardown of the customer's actual ABAP codebase, generates a custom **Migration Map** artifact, and walks out with both a deeper deal and a sales tool for the next prospect.
- **Directly monetizable** — each Migration Room is a packaged **$75–150K** engagement. Pays for itself the day it ships.
- Repeatable productized service motion the GTM team sells into every active enterprise opportunity.
- Each engagement produces a Migration Map — an anonymizable sales asset for every subsequent prospect.
- Builds reference customers fast — every Migration Room is a co-authored case study.
- **By year two, Nova owns** a repeatable, monetizable, customer-validated motion that produces revenue *and* reference assets in the same engagement. The most pipeline-tail-shaped of the four — the version Marcus buys directly.
