## What We Sell

### 1. Experience Strategy & Design
Audience-centered experience design that aligns events with business objectives. We define
the *what, why, and how* before execution begins — the strategic foundation, not the
floorplan. Attendee journey mapping, creative direction, visual identity, mood, experience
pillars, and environmental design (renders, floor plans, vignettes).

### 2. Event Production & Operations
End-to-end planning and delivery with calm, predictable execution. Venue sourcing and
contracting, vendor ecosystems, partner production schedules, workback timelines, budget
management, F&B, housing, room blocks, registration, staffing, and on-site logistics.
Complex programs made to feel effortless.

### 3. Keynote & Content Production
Full-scope keynote content from concept to curtain call: content strategy, scriptwriting,
video production (pre/post), presentation design, animation and motion graphics, rehearsal
support, and on-site show management. The team includes Executive Producer, Content Producer,
Creative Director, Scriptwriter, Presentation Designer/Animator, Video Director, Camera
Operator, Sound Mixer, and Video Editor.

### 4. Event Technology & Measurement
Technology selection, integration, and data capture that supports the experience — not
distracts from it. Registration platforms, digital signage, networking infrastructure,
demo deployment, and post-event measurement.

### 5. Signature Activation Pitches
Concept-led owned moments — councils, executive dinners, architect labs, documentary series
— built around a buyer's real pressure. Each pitch opens with a **named hero concept** as
the headline ask, with lighter pre-sketched shapes held as negotiation leverage.

### 6. Event-Led Growth Programs
Programs engineered so the room *is* the pipeline funnel: wired for signal capture →
attribution → GTM handoff. The event doubles as the brand moment **and** the revenue engine.

### 7. Borrowed-Audience Plays
Owned, invite-only moments staged *adjacent* to the third-party shows a client's buyers
already attend. Control the guest list, borrow the crowd, minimal acquisition lift.

## Operating Principles

### How we run projects
- **Four decks per program:** Internal Creative Deck (🟠), Client Creative Deck (🟣),
  Internal Graphics Deck, Client Graphics Deck. Slides created internally, moved to client
  when ready. BEING REVISED tag when updates are pending.
- **Top-of-week email** previews what the client can expect (OOO reminders, housekeeping,
  upcoming deadlines, call agendas). End-of-week email recaps.
- **Internal agendas reviewed by full team** before client version is updated.
- **Workback schedules** drive every program — milestones, dependencies, and deadlines
  tracked in the production schedule and partner production schedule.
- **Space Allocation Grid (SAG)** — the single source of truth for all function spaces,
  room assignments, and capacities.
- **Showbook** (Excel) for keynote programs — production schedule, crew list, comms
  assignments, speaker face sheets, ROS, and all content trackers.

### How we price
- All programs target **35% margin or higher**.
- Budget templates standardized across programs.
- SOWs generated per contractor per program from templates.
- Vendor cost tracking rigorous — the team actively finds savings (e.g., $5K saved on
  procurement by sourcing Amazon vs. local vendors for a DC event).

### How we source and staff
- **Senior-heavy model.** Small team, each person owns a real workstream.
  No layers, no account coordinators doing busywork.
- Contractors brought on per program via standardized SOWs.
- Staffing decisions tied to confirmed scope and budget — no speculative crewing.
- Production staff can flex across Intel programs (Connection → CES → Federal Summit)
  for continuity and efficiency.
